Houston, Texas Flood Hazard

Mapping Sea Level Rise

Houston is the 4th most populated city in the Unites States whose population is over 2 million people, with another 1 million expected to move there in the next 20 years. The most populated areas are the more newly developed neighborhoods to the northwest of city center. Warming oceans and melting glaciers are raising global sea levels, and for a city that sees an average of over fifty inches of rain per year, the flood risk is perhaps the highest in the country.

This sea level rise scenario points to an 83% risk of at least one flood over 1.5 meters taking place before 2050. Below is a mapped scenario of flooding due to sea level rise by meter, along with statistics from Surging Seas of the related risk.
